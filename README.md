# Human Fibroblast-Myeloid cell tissue atlas across lung, synovium, skin and heart

Lucy MacDonald<sup>1,2</sup>, Katerina Apostolopoulou<sup>3</sup>, Olympia Hardy<sup>1,2</sup>, Melpomeni Toitou<sup>3</sup>, Gabriela, Kania<sup>3</sup>, Przemyslaw Blyszczuk<sup>3</sup>, Raphael Micheroli<sup>3</sup>, Thomas D Otto<sup>1,2</sup>, Mariola Kurowska-Stolarska<sup>1,2§</sup> & Caroline Ospelt<sup>3§</sup>

§ contributed equally

1 Research into Inflammatory Arthritis Centre Versus Arthritis (RACE), University of Glasgow, United Kingdom

2 School of Infection & Immunity, University of Glasgow, United Kingdom

3 Center of Experimental Rheumatology, Department of Rheumatology, University Hospital Zurich, Zurich, Switzerland

We analyzed scRNAseq data from 14 public datasets, comprising heart, lung, skin, and synovium in both health and disease. We identified common and unique myeloid and stromal cell populations, providing insight into the conserved and tissue-specific roles of these cells in health and disease, aiding our understanding of tissue pathology.
